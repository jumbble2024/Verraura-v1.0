history of cardamom in India

Kerala's Roots


Cardamom is believed to have originated in the Western Ghats of southern India, and Kerala, known for its lush greenery and favorable climate, became one of the primary regions for the cultivation of cardamom. The spice has ancient roots in Kerala, where it was used not only for culinary purposes but also in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Spice Exchange


Kerala, strategically located on the southwestern coast of India, played a crucial role in the spice trade routes. Arab traders were among the first to establish a trade network, introducing cardamom to the Middle East. The spice became a significant part of Kerala's cultural and economic landscape, influencing both cuisine and traditional medicine practices.

Colonial Influence


During the colonial era, European powers, including the Portuguese, Dutch, and British, sought control over the spice trade. Kerala, being a major hub, became a center for European colonial activities. The British, in particular, recognized the economic potential of cardamom cultivation and established plantations in the hilly terrains of Kerala.
